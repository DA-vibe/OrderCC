01 The Mandate

Four weeks.
One standard.

For the next four weeks this course runs as one crew — a structure built to maintain accountability, communication, and support, so that no one drifts and everyone crosses the line together.

Accountability

Sixteen operators, present by 0755 hrs. Every status is known, every absence is communicated, and every morning is reported up the chain.

Communication

One clear route: member → Section Head → 2IC → Course Senior. Updates flow every morning; requests and concerns travel the same path.

Support

Every operator has an FTP — a partner for emotional and mental support, daily check-ins, and the certainty that someone is looking out for them.

05 Daily Protocol

The morning report.

Every day of the rotation follows the same rhythm — information rises through the chain and lands in the official report.

Step 01 · 0755 hrs

All present at school

Everyone is on deck by 0755 hrs. Anticipating a delay? Notify your Section Head immediately with an estimated time of arrival.

Step 02 · Section Heads

Morning update posted

Each Section Head posts an update on their members in the command group chat — appointments, absences, lateness, or any other concerns.

Step 03 · 2IC

Information verified

The 2IC confirms each update in the chat with an emoji — a thumbs-up or tick mark — before anything moves further up.

Step 04 · Course Senior

Consolidated into the official report

The Course Senior consolidates the verified updates and includes them in the official report. Uncommunicated status becomes a recorded absence.

Routing rule: any new request or concern goes to your Section Head first, before being escalated further up the chain.

07 Transmission

Word from the Course Senior.

The standing brief for the rotation. Read it once, live it daily.

FROM · COURSE SENIOR (CS) · S3 DA ◈ Standing Brief

Good day, everyone. As I will be serving as the Course Senior (CS) for the next four weeks, I would like to establish a structure that will help us maintain accountability, communication, and support within the course.

I have organized the course into four sections, with four individuals in each section. These groupings have been made based on my assessment of your strengths to ensure balanced and effective teams.

Additionally, I would like everyone to have an FTP. The purpose of this is to provide emotional and mental support, facilitate daily check-ins, and ensure everyone has someone looking out for them.

Each section will have a Section Head (3IC). Moving forward:

  • Section Heads are to provide a morning update in the newly created group chat regarding their members, including appointments, absences, lateness, or any other concerns.
  • The 2IC will verify the information in the chat with an emoji like a thumbs-up or tick mark.
  • I will then consolidate and include the updates in the official report.

Please ensure that everyone is present at school by 0755 hrs. If you anticipate being late, notify your Section Head immediately and provide an estimated time of arrival (ETA). Failure to communicate your status may result in your absence being reflected in the report. Any new requests or concerns should first be routed through your respective Section Head before being escalated further.

Ultimately, we are all adults and professionals. I trust everyone to exercise good judgment, take responsibility for their actions, support one another, and, most importantly, stay safe.

S3 DA Course Senior · Serial 2
